# Compliance Rules for AI Content Creators (China, 2026)

## Tool Recommendation Restrictions

In mainland China, the following tools **cannot be directly recommended** (require VPN / overseas phone number):
- ChatGPT / OpenAI family
- Claude / Anthropic family
- Gemini / Google AI family
- Perplexity
- Midjourney

**Only recommend domestically available tools:** DeepSeek, Doubao (ByteDance), Kimi (Moonshot), Tongyi Qianwen (Alibaba), Wenxin Yiyan (Baidu), Zhipu ChatGLM, etc.

If an overseas tool is central to the topic (e.g., "Global AI tool comparison"), label it: "This tool is not directly accessible in mainland China."

## Xiaohongshu AI Content Labeling (Since Feb 2026)

- AI-generated/synthesized images, text, and video must proactively carry an AI label
- Unlabeled AI content gets reach-restricted
- Tutorials on "removing AI labels" are strictly prohibited
- Talking-head videos (real person on camera) are NOT classified as AI content, but AI-generated visuals or text shown in the video should be noted

## National Regulation

"Administrative Measures for Labeling AI-Generated Synthetic Content" (effective Sep 2025) requires both explicit + implicit dual labeling for AI-generated content. Content creators covering AI tools should naturally mention "this was generated by AI" — both for compliance and audience trust.

## Sensitive Topics to Avoid

- Don't debate whether AI regulation is right or wrong
- Don't compare US-China AI development gaps (politically sensitive)
- Don't promote "AI replacing human workers" narrative (reframe as "AI-assisted efficiency")
- Don't recommend using AI to mass-produce content for profit (Xiaohongshu actively penalizes this)
